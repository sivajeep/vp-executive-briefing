Industry Fit

Industries I'm
Built For

St. Louis is not simply where I am based — it is where my family has put down roots, where my children are growing up, and where I am deeply invested in the economic vitality and infrastructure that powers this region. That rootedness shapes how I lead: with long-term commitment, not short-term ambition.

My entire career has been built inside one of the world's most demanding regulated environments — global payments. The industries I am best positioned to lead share a common DNA with that experience: high-availability infrastructure where failure is not an option, stringent multi-jurisdictional compliance obligations, a customer base of millions who expect reliability as a baseline and digital innovation as a differentiator, and federated organizational complexity that requires both central discipline and business-unit autonomy.

Whether the mission-critical system is a payment network, an energy grid, a healthcare platform, or a financial exchange — the leadership principles are the same. Build for resilience. Modernize without disruption. Invest with transparency. Develop the people who make it all run.

The next chapter of my career is about bringing two decades of regulated-industry technology leadership to an organization where reliability, innovation, and community impact converge at scale.

Leadership Philosophy

How I Lead.
How Teams Win.

01
Technology Serves the Business
Every architecture decision, every platform investment, every vendor negotiation begins and ends with a business outcome. I don't build technology for its own sake — I build capabilities that unlock revenue, reduce cost, and serve customers better.
02
Reliability Is Non-Negotiable
In critical infrastructure — whether a payment network, an energy system, or a financial exchange — failure has real-world consequences for real people. I have built operational cultures where reliability is the first principle, and where teams are measured, trained, and rewarded accordingly.
03
Transparency Builds Trust
Boards and CFOs don't need to understand every technical detail — but they deserve complete honesty about costs, risks, and tradeoffs. I communicate with clarity and conviction, and I never hide hard truths behind technical complexity.
04
People Are the Platform
The best technology strategy fails without the right people, culture, and leadership pipeline. I invest deeply in talent development, build environments where engineers do their best work, and treat succession planning as a strategic obligation — not an afterthought.
